Structure and Scale

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy with a diameter of about 100,000–200,000 light-years and contains 100–400 billion stars. At its center lies a supermassive black hole known as Sagittarius A*. The solar system is located on the Orion Arm, approximately 27,000 light-years from the Galactic Center.

Its name is derived from its appearance as a dim, glowing band arching across the night sky, made up of stars that cannot be individually distinguished by the naked eye.
